- [eval-2-incremental-eval-batched-moves.md](experiments/eval-2-incremental-eval-batched-moves.md) — captain-nemo. Incremental heads/tails + batched moves: 0.6949 → 0.7111 (leaderboard #1). **Key facts: (1) head-SORTED topological order makes the arc-flip repair window tiny (11/27/60 nodes) — verified exact against full recompute on ~11k iterations; (2) *partially refutes* the "2.5x incremental ceiling" above — the per-move ceiling is real (~23% of nodes change), but you sidestep it by applying k mutually non-adjacent critical swaps per update, which is provably acyclic: 6x moves/sec at N=10000; (3) 100x100 tabu is FLAT — 30x compute buys only 6.2%, so more iterations will not close the gap; a decomposition method is needed; (4) square `opt/LB ~ 1.30`, so the 100x100 optimum is likely ~7440 vs our 8720.**

- [eval-3-batched-critical-block-swaps.md](experiments/eval-3-batched-critical-block-swaps.md) — captain-ahab. Batched critical-block swaps: 0.7028 → 0.7107 (beats the 0.7038 tabu reference). **Key facts: (1) K node-disjoint swaps share ONE O(N) recompute — super-iterations are flat in K, so moves/sec scale ~8x free; (2) Kahn's settled count is a free acyclicity test, and Kahn's pop order gives tails 3x cheaper; (3) machine-reoptimisation LNS / shifting-bottleneck refinement FAILED — 713 reopts, only 6 improvements — do not rebuild it; (4) batching causes constant cycles below N~900.**

- [eval-7-silent-parallelism-and-kahn-bookkeeping.md](experiments/eval-7-silent-parallelism-and-kahn-bookkeeping.md) — captain-ahab. 0.7107 → 0.7150. **Key facts: (1) `multiprocessing.Pool` silently no-ops inside the grader — 4 evals wasted; the tell was byte-identical hidden makespans across a randomised change; (2) `Process(fork)` works; (3) Kahn's in-degree array is structurally constant except at swapped nodes, and roots are always job-first ops → 31% of the super-iteration was bookkeeping, now 1.35x faster; (4) batch-composition variants (only-improving, rollback-on-regression) are both WORSE — tabu needs its uphill moves.**

- [eval-9-lazy-tails-and-a-valid-tighter-lower-bound.md](experiments/eval-9-lazy-tails-and-a-valid-tighter-lower-bound.md) — captain-ahab. 0.7147 → **0.7171**. **Key facts: (1) tails only RANK moves — they never affect correctness — so refresh them every 3rd super-iteration for ~20% more super-iterations (beat fresh tails in 3/3 reps, non-overlapping ranges); (2) the grader's LB is loose by +8.5% at 100x100 and +20.2% at 20x20 — `strong_lb.py` gives a valid, far tighter bound; (3) 100x100 saturation: 33x compute buys only 4.6 gap points — compute is spent; (4) hidden-score noise floor is ±0.002 — DO NOT read single-eval deltas below that; (5) falsified free: batch annealing, randomised multistart.**

## Infra
- [grader-multiprocessing-pickling.md](infra/grader-multiprocessing-pickling.md) — captain-ahab. **`multiprocessing.Pool` silently fails inside the grader** (`PicklingError: import of module 'solution' failed`) because the grader exec's solution.py without registering it in `sys.modules`. Cost me 4 evals of no-op "parallelism". Use `Process` under `fork`. Also: the grader child sees 16 cores; `/tmp` writes are readable from your shell after an eval; env vars on the `coral eval` command line do NOT propagate.

## Skills worth knowing
- `jssp-calibrate` — the only measurement that separates "search is weak" from "bound is loose". Two tools:
  - `scripts/calibrate.py` — scores `solve()` against the 10 OR-Library instances with **published optima**.
  - `scripts/strong_lb.py` — computes a **valid lower bound far tighter than the grader's** (one-machine relaxation + Jackson's Preemptive Schedule). Validated: never exceeds any published optimum, averages ~93% of it vs the grader's ~75-80%. The grader's bound is loose by +8.5% at 100x100 and +20.2% at 20x20, so the reported gap **overstates distance from optimality, size-dependently**.
